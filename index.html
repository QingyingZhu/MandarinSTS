Mandarin Speech-To-Singing Synthesis System

A Mandarin Chinese Speech-To-Singing Symthesis System

Qinging Zhu, Minchuan Chen, Shaojun Wang, Jing Xiao

Ping An Technology Co. Ltd

A Mandarin Chinese Speech-To-Singing synthesis system is described in this paper. Given a musical score of a song and a speaking voice reading its lyrics, the system converts the speaking voice into a singing voice using three control models: the F0 model, the duration model and the spectral envelope model. The F0 model generates the singing F0 contour from a musical score, while adding unique elements to the singing voice. Additional optimization of unvoiced consonant is done to adapt Mandarin Chinese. The duration model adjusts the duration of a Chinese syllable based on the duration of its musical note. This paper also explores proper way to assign duration of a syllable to its composing phoneme. The spectrum model converts a speaking voice into a singing voice by controlling the singing formant of vowels.

Demo 2: Synthesizing Singing Voice From Synthesized Speaking Voice

For each lyrics phrase, a speaking voice of the lyrics is first synthesized by a text-to-speech (TTS) system. Then, with a corresponding musical socre, the syntheisized speaking voice is further transformed into a singing voice by the proposed Mandarin STS system. One Female and one male TTS model was trained and used for this demo.
